New Democratic Policy Platform 2003

Practical Solutions for America's Economic Crisis

Fiscal Responsibility & Debt Reduction ▼

Emergency Deficit Response Package

  • Deficit Reduction Target: Cut deficit from $705 billion (2003) to $400 billion by 2006 through a combination of spending restraint and economic growth.
  • Debt Service Stabilization: Cap debt service payments at current 4.51% of GDP through refinancing shorter-term debt.
  • Budget Process Reform: Institute statutory PAYGO rules requiring all new spending or tax cuts to be offset within the same fiscal year.
  • Automatic Sequestration: If deficit exceeds 4% of GDP, trigger automatic 2% across-the-board cuts to discretionary spending (excluding Social Security/Medicare).

Tax Code Modernization

  • Middle Class Tax Relief: Reduce 25-50k bracket from 20% to 18%, 50-75k bracket from 25% to 23%.
  • Earned Income Tax Credit Expansion: Increase EITC by 25% for families with children, phasing out at higher income levels.
  • Small Business Expensing: Allow businesses under $10M revenue to immediately expense equipment purchases up to $100,000 annually.
  • Capital Gains Indexation: Index capital gains to inflation for assets held over 2 years to encourage long-term investment.
Economic Recovery & Growth ▼

Technology Sector Stabilization

  • R&D Tax Credit Enhancement: Increase R&D tax credit from 20% to 30% for companies investing in domestic research facilities.
  • Tech Worker Retraining: $2 billion fund for displaced tech workers to retrain in healthcare, education, and infrastructure sectors.
  • Broadband Infrastructure: Federal matching funds for states to expand high-speed internet access, creating 200,000 construction jobs.
  • Patent Reform: Streamline patent approval process and reduce fees for small inventors and startups.

Manufacturing Renaissance

  • Made in America Tax Credit: 5% tax credit for manufacturers who maintain 75% domestic production.
  • Trade Enforcement Unit: Create a 500-person trade enforcement division to investigate dumping and currency manipulation.
  • Manufacturing Extension Partnership: Triple funding for MEP to help small manufacturers adopt new technologies and improve efficiency.
  • Infrastructure Jobs Program: $50 billion over 3 years for highway, bridge, and port improvements prioritizing American-made materials.

Small Business Support

  • SBA Loan Guarantee Expansion: Increase SBA loan guarantees from 75% to 85% for loans under $500,000.
  • Small Business Health Insurance: Allow small businesses to pool together for health insurance purchasing power.
  • Regulatory Relief: Exempt businesses under 50 employees from new federal regulations for the first 2 years of operation.
  • One-Stop Business Registration: Federal grants to states creating single online portals for all business licensing.
Workforce Development ▼

Skills for the 21st Century

  • Community College Partnership: $5 billion to partner community colleges with local employers for job-specific training programs.
  • Apprenticeship Tax Credit: $2,000 per apprentice tax credit for employers, with the goal of 500,000 new apprenticeships by 2006.
  • Portable Benefits Pilot: Test programs in 10 states allowing benefits to follow workers between jobs.
  • Career Services Modernization: Upgrade unemployment offices to provide job matching, skills assessment, and training referrals.

Education Reform

  • Teacher Quality Initiative: $3 billion for performance bonuses and student loan forgiveness for teachers in high-need schools.
  • School Accountability: Tie 20% of federal education funding to measurable improvements in graduation rates and test scores.
  • Vocational Education Revival: Restore federal support for trade schools and technical training programs.
  • Higher Education Affordability: Allow student loan refinancing at federal rates, cap loan payments at 10% of income.
Healthcare Cost Control ▼

Market-Based Healthcare Reform

  • Health Savings Account Expansion: Increase HSA contribution limits to $5,000 individual/$10,000 family, allow rollover.
  • Prescription Drug Competition: Allow Medicare to negotiate drug prices for high-volume medications.
  • Medical Liability Reform: Cap non-economic damages at $250,000, establish medical courts for malpractice cases.
  • Price Transparency: Require hospitals and clinics to post standard procedure costs online.

Family Health Security

  • Children's Health Insurance: Expand CHIP to families earning up to 250% of poverty level.
  • Preventive Care Investment: Tax credits for employers offering comprehensive wellness programs.
  • Mental Health Parity: Require insurance coverage for mental health equal to physical health coverage.
  • Rural Health Access: Loan forgiveness for doctors and nurses serving in underserved areas for 5+ years.
Social Security & Retirement Security ▼

Long-Term Solvency Reforms

  • Retirement Age Adjustment: Gradually increase full retirement age from 65 to 67 over a 20-year period.
  • Benefit Formula Adjustment: Reduce benefits for high earners (top 5%) while protecting current and near-retirees.
  • Private Account Option: Allow workers under 35 to invest 2% of payroll taxes in regulated index funds.
  • Disability Reform: Strengthen work incentives and vocational rehabilitation for disabled beneficiaries.

Pension Protection

  • 401(k) Automatic Enrollment: Require employers to automatically enroll workers in retirement plans with an opt-out option.
  • Pension Insurance Strengthening: Increase PBGC premiums on underfunded plans to protect retiree benefits.
  • Small Business Retirement: Simplified retirement plans for businesses under 100 employees with reduced administrative costs.
  • Financial Literacy: Mandatory retirement planning education in high schools and community colleges.
Trade & Globalization ▼

Fair Trade Enforcement

  • Currency Manipulation Response: Authority to impose tariffs on countries artificially depressing currency values.
  • Labor Standards Compliance: Trade agreements must include enforceable worker safety and wage protections.
  • Trade Adjustment Assistance: Extend TAA to service workers, increase benefits to 70% of previous wages.
  • Buy American Preference: 10% price preference for American goods in federal procurement contracts.

Immigration Reform

  • High-Skill Immigration: Increase H-1B visas for STEM graduates from American universities.
  • Temporary Worker Program: Guest worker program for agriculture and construction with labor protections.
  • Border Security Technology: Invest in electronic monitoring and processing systems rather than physical barriers.
  • Earned Legalization: Path to permanent residence for undocumented immigrants who pay taxes and learn English.
Energy Independence ▼

Domestic Energy Production

  • Strategic Petroleum Reserve: Maintain a 90-day supply while encouraging domestic exploration.
  • Natural Gas Infrastructure: Federal support for pipeline construction and LNG terminals.
  • Nuclear Power Renaissance: Streamline licensing for new nuclear plants meeting enhanced safety standards.
  • Clean Coal Technology: Research funding for carbon capture and clean coal technologies.

Alternative Energy Incentives

  • Renewable Energy Tax Credits: Extend and expand tax credits for wind, solar, and geothermal projects.
  • Energy Efficiency Standards: Appliance and building efficiency standards projected to save consumers $20 billion annually.
  • Hybrid Vehicle Credits: Tax credits for purchasing fuel-efficient vehicles, with higher credits for American-made models.
  • Grid Modernization: Public-private partnerships to upgrade electrical transmission infrastructure.
Government Reform ▼

Performance-Based Government

  • Program Assessment Rating Tool: Systematic evaluation of all federal programs with public scorecards.
  • Zero-Based Budgeting: Require agencies to justify all spending from zero baseline every 3 years.
  • Federal Workforce Reform: Link federal employee raises to performance metrics and agency results.
  • Sunset Clauses: All new programs automatically expire after 7 years unless reauthorized with proven results.

Democratic Accountability

  • Campaign Finance Reform: Voluntary public financing for congressional candidates who meet fundraising thresholds.
  • Lobbying Disclosure: Real-time electronic filing of lobbying contacts and expenditures.
  • Ethics Enforcement: Independent ethics office with authority to investigate and prosecute violations.
  • Redistricting Standards: Federal guidelines requiring compact, contiguous districts following political boundaries.
Regional Economic Development ▼

Addressing Regional Challenges

  • Cambridge Region: $2 billion manufacturing revitalization fund focusing on aerospace and defense industries.
  • Superior Region: Housing construction tax credits and infrastructure investment to support manufacturing recovery.
  • Columbia Region: Expanded Earned Income Tax Credit and job training programs targeting poverty reduction.
  • Heartland Region: Rural health clinic expansion and telemedicine infrastructure development.
  • Yellowstone Region: Agricultural diversification programs and rural broadband expansion.
  • Austin Region: Oil revenue stabilization fund and economic diversification incentives.
  • Phoenix Region: Grid reliability investment and homeland security technology development.

Urban-Rural Partnership

  • Main Street Investment: Tax credits for businesses investing in downtown revitalization in communities under 50,000.
  • Agricultural Competitiveness: Crop insurance reform and support for value-added agricultural processing.
  • Rural Broadband Initiative: Federal matching funds for rural internet infrastructure development.
  • Urban Empowerment Zones: Tax incentives for businesses locating in distressed urban areas.
National Security & Defense ▼

Defense Efficiency

  • Defense Acquisition Reform: Competitive bidding requirements for all contracts over $100 million.
  • Base Realignment: Close 25% of overseas bases while maintaining strategic capabilities.
  • Military Healthcare Integration: Combine military and VA healthcare systems for improved efficiency.
  • Defense Manufacturing: Maintain domestic production capacity for critical defense technologies.

Homeland Security

  • Port Security: Container inspection technology and security upgrades at major ports.
  • Information Sharing: Integrated database system for intelligence agencies with privacy protections.
  • Emergency Preparedness: Federal standards for state and local emergency response capabilities.
  • Critical Infrastructure: Public-private partnerships to protect power grids, water systems, and communications.
